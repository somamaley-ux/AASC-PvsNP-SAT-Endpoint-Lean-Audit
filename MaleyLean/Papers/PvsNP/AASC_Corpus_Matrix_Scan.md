## Purpose

The live P vs NP bridge needs non-SAT AASC support for this shape:

> A nondegenerate same-scope admissibility-bearing classifier cannot survive as
> an independent same-domain separator. If it stays inside the unique
> admissible interior, it instantiates the kernel by necessity. If it tries to
> act from outside or import authority, it crosses the AMetric boundary, which
> is forbidden.

The matrix contains strong source support for the shape, but the relevant rows
are marked `Not verified in this pass` and do not list a Lean file in
`Lean_file_or_repo`. Therefore they are source anchors for the next Lean bridge,
not already-verified Lean facts.

## Supplemental CNF4 Solver-Control Support

Source package:

`G:/AASC corpus may 7/Core spine/ZZZNew Work/ZSubmission versions/Erkenntnis/Numerical forcing ARC/CNF4 Invariant Solver Structures/TEX/CNF4__Invariant_Solver_Structures_for_Constructive_Numerical_Forcing.zip`

Unpacked local scan target:

`.codex-work/cnf4_invariant_solver_structures/CNF4_Invariant_Solver_Structures.tex`

CNF4 is not SAT-specific and does not prove the classifier bridge by itself.
It is nevertheless useful supplemental support for the solver/resource side of
the lower-bound residual: formal solvability, a solver output, or a resource
bound cannot be promoted into an admissible same-domain separator unless the
solver certificate is already standing-bearing, quotient-stable,
non-selectorial, non-totalizing, and projection-admissible.

Relevant CNF4 objects:

- Solver domain inheritance, lines 239-247: a raw coordinate space, formal
  completion, empirical fitting family, or externally extended operator domain
  is not a solver domain merely because equations can be written on it.
- Valid solver certificate, lines 280-285: certificate components must be
  declared, dependency-closed, calibration-prior, standing-related,
  quotient-compatible, and non-selectorial.
- Solver Factorization Theorem, lines 306-314: every admissible solver factors
  through standing-preserving reduction, quotient-stable operator action, and
  admissible projection.
- Projection Invariance Principle and Criterion, lines 321-340: an admissible
  projection cannot choose representatives, endpoints, branches, bases, or
  normalizations, and cannot upgrade output strength.
- Projection is not readout by convention, lines 342-352: projection becomes a
  selector unless the extraction is fixed by standing-bearing data.
- Solver Admissibility Theorem, lines 387-400: solver strengthening requires a
  valid certificate and excludes target import, totalization, hidden selection,
  unclosed obstruction branches, and projection-as-final-selector.
- Formal solvability non-sufficiency, lines 403-405: solution existence does
  not imply solver forcing.
- No Solver-Selector Theorem, lines 411-416: choosing from a finite, bounded,
  overlap-reduced, transport-relative, or holonomy-valued class is inadmissible
  unless the choice criterion is already standing-fixed.
- No Solver Totalization, lines 427-435: solving by domain extension is a
  different-domain solution unless the extension is already part of the
  standing carrier ledger.
- Solver-Strength Non-Promotion, lines 745-751: a solver cannot promote finite
  to exact, quotient-singleton to raw exact, bounded to discrete,
  transport-relative to same-domain, obstruction to positive selection,
  calibration-compatible to forced, or formal existence to admissible value
  without a certificate for that stronger status.
- CNF4 Failure-Route Exhaustion and Registry Theorem, lines 1168-1184: failed
  solver attempts are normalized by route, and no solver output may be promoted
  beyond its certificate.

Bridge role:

CNF4 helps block a tempting residual laundering move: treating finite
computation, solver existence, formal minimization, genericity, endpoint choice,
or projection/readout as if it were an admissible same-domain separating
classifier. In the P vs NP bridge this supports:

- `CnfSameDomainSeparatorWouldImportSelector R`;
- the anti-totalization reading of `CnfBoundarySelectorImported R`;
- the claim that an alleged lower-bound residual must either be already
  standing-fixed/bookkeeping, or else it imports selector/projection/domain
  authority and falls to the AMetric boundary route.

It does not replace the primary same-scope classifier uniqueness source
(`AASC4-P06-THM-008`) or the AMetric non-crossing source
(`AASC4-P24-THM-024`). It sharpens the solver/resource subcase.

### CNF1

Best support:

- numerical readiness does not imply constructive forcing;
- formula, fit, finite remainder, dimensional analysis, optimization,
  anthropic selection, generic constraint solving, and empirical success do
  not become prediction without a carrier-preserving, witness-preserving,
  quotient-stable, selector-free forcing map;
- a finite typed value remains a finite class unless an additional admissible
  selector collapses it;
- prediction-strength non-promotion forbids reporting weaker statuses as
  stronger statuses.

Bridge role:

CNF1 supplies the general no-hidden-selector/no-promotion grammar. For P vs NP,
it blocks treating a displayed algorithm, fit, formal construction, or resource
description as an admissible separating authority unless it factors through the
declared fixed-domain ledger.

### CNF2

Best support:

- finite collapse is not exact selection;
- enumerable does not imply finite, and finite does not imply exact;
- exact selection requires a separate CNF1-admissible uniqueness or selection
  condition;
- a termination witness is not a label on a finite table but an audit of the
  reduction process;
- finite collapse requires quotient exhaustion, obstruction completeness,
  admissible closure, refinement rigidity, sound termination, and no hidden
  selector/calibration reversal.

Bridge role:

CNF2 is the cleanest support for the user's finite-resource point. It blocks
the move from finite computation or finite survivor structure to a selected
same-domain endpoint. In the P vs NP bridge, it supports the claim that finite
resources do not themselves produce a separator unless the selector/uniqueness
burden has already been discharged.

### CNF3

Best support:

- overlap is not raw set intersection;
- compatibility is not coincidence;
- non-singleton overlap cannot be reported as exact;
- quotient-singleton exactness is not raw representative exactness;
- no hidden overlap selector;
- raw intersection and engineered quotient agreement are insufficient.

Bridge role:

CNF3 supports the same-domain comparison side: two constructions can only force
coincidence when they share a witness-complete overlap, quotient-compatible
comparison, closed disagreement ledger, and calibration-prior dependency graph.
It blocks raw agreement, shared notation, or overlapping survivor lists from
being promoted into an admissible classifier.

### CNF9

Best support:

- capstone aggregation cannot strengthen status;
- every registry row must preserve the strongest status actually certified;
- raw exactness requires no unresolved quotient, representative, basis, scale,
  phase, transport, normalization, endpoint, scheme, or calibration freedom;
- obstruction outputs and failed evaluations are separated;
- future exactness claims require separate certificate chains.

Bridge role:

CNF9 is useful as a final audit discipline: collecting many partial closures
cannot itself close the P vs NP bridge. The final no-independent-separator
claim must still be backed by the specific same-scope classifier and AMetric
boundary source package.

## Conclusion

The matrix is helpful. It gives a clean non-SAT source spine:

1. fixed-domain comparison class;
2. same-scope operator exhaustion;
3. no internalized import;
4. same-scope classifier uniqueness;
5. AMetric boundary non-crossing.

That spine is exactly the AASC-language support needed for the remaining
classifier/source bridge. The present limitation is formal, not conceptual: the
CSV rows are source anchors with no listed Lean artifact, so they should be
turned into an explicit source package or theorem ledger before being counted
as an unconditional Lean discharge.

CNF4 adds a useful supplemental spine for the solver/resource reading of the
residual: solver existence and finite/resource-bounded computation do not
themselves supply admissible separator authority. They must factor through a
standing-bearing certificate, and otherwise become hidden selector, projection,
totalization, calibration, endpoint-import, or scope-change attempts.

The CNF family scan strengthens this: CNF1 supplies no-promotion/no-hidden
selector discipline, CNF2 supplies finite-resource non-selection, CNF3 supplies
overlap/coincidence non-promotion, CNF4 supplies solver-control
non-totalization, and CNF9 supplies capstone non-strengthening. Together these
are enough source support for the anti-laundering half of the final bridge.
They still do not replace the need to instantiate the same-scope
no-independent-classifier theorem for SAT.
